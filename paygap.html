Exploring the U.S. Gender Salary Gap by Occupation Through the Public Dataset

Nearly every occupation in the United States experiences a gender wage gap. In 2017, it was commonly known that women were paid on average 82 cents for every dollar a man earns. However, based on the controlled data, the gap varies significantly by field. Women are paid almost half of what men are paid (56.3%) in some legal occupations such as financial clerks, while architectural and engineering managers experience almost no gap (99.9%). Considering the complexity of the gender pay gap issue, this data-driven dashboard analyzes the gender wage gap by occupation through reported earnings by gender using data from the American Community Survey.
